Help & FAQ

  • How do I subscribe to Pro? Tap any locked feature to open the Pro paywall. Choose a plan (Monthly, Yearly, or Lifetime), confirm with Face ID / Touch ID, and Pro unlocks instantly across the app.
  • How do I cancel my subscription? Open iPhone Settings → tap your name → Subscriptions → Make My Day → Cancel Subscription. You keep Pro until the end of the current billing period.
  • I bought Pro on another device — how do I restore it? On the new device, open the Pro paywall and tap Restore Purchases. Sign in with the same Apple ID used for the original purchase.
  • Where is my data stored? All your data lives locally on your device. We never upload your tasks, transactions, or habits to a server. Optional iCloud sync (Pro) uses your private iCloud — only you can read it.
  • How do I request a refund? Refunds are handled by Apple, not us. Visit reportaproblem.apple.com, find your Make My Day purchase, and submit the request.
  • The app crashed. What do I do? Quit the app completely, reopen it, and try again. If the problem persists, email us with your iOS version and what you were doing — we usually ship a fix within 48 hours.
  • Why does the app ask for permissions? We only ask for what we actually need. Notifications power task and habit reminders. Camera/Photos lets you attach receipts to transactions. Calendar access lets us read birthdays into the Special Days tool. Each permission is optional — denying it disables only that one feature.

About Make My Day

One app. Five productive lives.

Tasks, reminders, habits, money tracking, and 35+ smart tools — all in a single, beautifully designed iOS app. No more switching between five different apps to plan your day.

Privacy first.

Your data stays on your device. We don't upload, sell, or share your tasks, transactions, or habits. The only data we collect is anonymous, aggregated usage analytics — no personal identifiers.

Built for India.

Indian-rupee defaults, region-aware date and time formats, financial calculators tuned for Indian use cases (EMI, gratuity, gold/silver), and pricing designed for the Indian market.
